# Track E — Behavioral & Communication (Googleyness + Leadership)

**Target**: Pass Google's Googleyness & Leadership interview dimension
**Weight**: ~25% of Google's hiring signal — don't skip this track
**Method**: Story crafting → Practice articulation → Mock responses

---

## Why This Matters

Google evaluates:
1. **Googleyness**: Collaboration, navigating ambiguity, handling disagreements, learning mindset
2. **Leadership**: Influencing without authority, mentoring, driving outcomes, making tough calls
3. **Communication**: Clear thinking, structured responses, appropriate detail level

Many strong coders fail at Google because they neglect this dimension.

---

## How Each Session Works

**Story sessions** (~30-45 min):
1. Identify a story from your experience
2. Write it in STAR format (Situation, Task, Action, Result)
3. Time yourself telling it aloud (target: 2-3 min per story)
4. Refine — cut fluff, sharpen the action/result

**Practice sessions** (~30 min):
1. Pick a common behavioral question
2. Choose the right story from your bank
3. Tell it aloud. Time it. <3 min.
4. Add a "what I learned" or "what I'd do differently" ending

## Module 1: Core Stories (Sessions 1-8)

### Session 1 — Identify Your Impact Stories
**Exercise**: List your top 10 professional accomplishments across your career.
For each, write one sentence: "I did X which resulted in Y (measured by Z)."

**Filter to top 6** that demonstrate:
- Technical leadership
- Ambiguity navigation
- Cross-team collaboration
- Tough trade-off decisions
- Failure + recovery
- Mentoring/growth

### Session 2 — STAR Format Deep Dive
**For your #1 story (biggest impact), write full STAR:**
- **Situation**: 2-3 sentences. What was the context? What was at stake?
- **Task**: What was YOUR specific responsibility? (Not the team's — yours)
- **Action**: 3-5 bullet points of what YOU did. Be specific. Name technologies, approaches, decisions.
- **Result**: Quantified outcome. Revenue? Users? Time saved? Reliability improved?

**Practice**: Tell it aloud in under 3 minutes. Record if possible.

### Session 3 — Story #2: Navigating Ambiguity
**Write STAR for**: A time you had to make progress without clear direction.
**Focus on**: How you broke down the ambiguity, who you consulted, how you made decisions
**Google signal**: Can this person be productive without hand-holding? Do they seek information proactively?

### Session 4 — Story #3: Technical Disagreement
**Write STAR for**: A time you disagreed with a technical approach and influenced the outcome.
**Focus on**: How you presented data, how you listened to the other side, how you resolved it
**Google signal**: Can this person disagree respectfully? Do they use data over ego?
**Important**: Show you COULD be wrong. Show flexibility. Never sound like "I was right and they were wrong."

### Session 5 — Story #4: Cross-Team Collaboration
**Write STAR for**: A time you drove a project that required coordination across multiple teams.
**Focus on**: How you built alignment, how you handled different priorities, how you tracked progress
**Google signal**: Can this person lead without authority? Can they get buy-in?

### Session 6 — Story #5: Failure & Recovery
**Write STAR for**: Something that went wrong (production incident, missed deadline, wrong decision).
**Focus on**: What you learned, how you recovered, what you changed afterward
**Google signal**: Is this person self-aware? Do they learn from mistakes? Are they honest?
**Important**: Own it. Don't blame others. Show growth.

### Session 7 — Story #6: Mentoring & Growing Others
**Write STAR for**: A time you helped someone else grow (junior engineer, peer, cross-functional partner).
**Focus on**: What you taught, how you adapted to their level, what was the outcome for THEM
**Google signal**: Does this person make the team better? Do they invest in others?

### Session 8 — Story Review & Polish
**For all 6 stories**:
1. Time each one aloud. All must be <3 min.
2. Check: Does each have a quantified result?
3. Check: Is YOUR role clear (not "we did X" but "I did X")?
4. Prepare 1-sentence "hook" for each: a grabber that sets the scene quickly.

## Module 2: Common Questions (Sessions 9-14)

### Session 9 — "Tell me about yourself"
**Craft your 2-min intro:**
- Current role + what you do (30 sec)
- Most impressive accomplishment (30 sec)
- Why Google + what excites you about the role (30 sec)
- What you bring that's unique (30 sec)

**Practice**: Tell it aloud 3 times. Cut it to 90 seconds.

### Session 10 — Leadership Without Authority
**Common questions**: "How did you influence a decision?" "How did you get buy-in?"
**Map to your stories**: Which story best answers this? Prepare a version.
**Key framing**: I proposed X. I gathered data showing Y. I addressed concern Z. The team adopted it.

### Session 11 — Handling Conflict
**Common questions**: "Tell me about a disagreement." "How do you handle pushback?"
**Map to your stories**: Use your Technical Disagreement story. Prepare variants.
**Key framing**: I listened, I understood their perspective, I proposed a compromise/experiment, we resolved it.

### Session 12 — Dealing with Ambiguity
**Common questions**: "What do you do when requirements are unclear?" "How do you prioritize?"
**Map to your stories**: Use your Navigating Ambiguity story.
**Key framing**: I identified the unknowns, I proposed lightweight experiments, I iterated based on feedback.

### Session 13 — Growth Mindset & Failure
**Common questions**: "Tell me about a mistake." "What would you do differently?"
**Map to your stories**: Use your Failure & Recovery story.
**Key framing**: I made X mistake. It caused Y impact. I learned Z. Now I do W differently.
**Important**: Never say "I can't think of a failure." Always have one ready.

### Session 14 — Why Google? + Career Goals
**Prepare answers for**:
- "Why Google?" (specific, authentic, not generic)
- "Why SSE?" (why this level, what you bring)
- "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?" (growth at Google, not leaving Google)
- "What's your ideal team?" (describe collaborative, impactful environment)

## Module 3: Interview Simulation (Sessions 15-20)

### Session 15 — Mock Behavioral #1
**Format**: Pick 4 questions randomly. Answer each in <3 min. Time yourself.
**Questions bank**:
- "Tell me about a project you led."
- "How did you handle a production incident?"
- "Describe a time you had to learn something quickly."
- "Tell me about a time you simplified something complex."

### Session 16 — Mock Behavioral #2
**Format**: Same as above, different questions:
- "How do you mentor junior engineers?"
- "Tell me about a time you had to say no to a stakeholder."
- "Describe your biggest technical decision and its trade-offs."
- "How do you handle technical debt?"

### Session 17 — Googleyness Deep Dive
**Study**: What Google means by Googleyness
**Core traits**: Enjoys fun, comfortable with ambiguity, collaborative, pushes back on bureaucracy, questions assumptions, bias for action
**Practice**: For each trait, map to a story from your experience.
**Anti-patterns**: Arrogance, rigidity, "my way or the highway", blaming others

### Session 18 — Mock with Follow-ups
**Format**: Tell one story. Then answer 3 follow-up probes:
- "What would you do differently?"
- "How did the other person react?"
- "What was the measurable outcome?"
- "What did you learn from this?"
**Practice**: Follow-ups are where most people stumble. Prepare deep answers.

### Session 19 — Questions to Ask YOUR Interviewers
**Prepare 5-7 thoughtful questions for Google interviewers:**
- About the team's biggest challenge
- About how decisions are made
- About the tech stack and trade-offs
- About growth opportunities
- About what "great" looks like in this role

**Important**: These questions signal your thinking level. Make them specific.

### Session 20 — Final Behavioral Synthesis
**Complete checklist**:
- [ ] 6 polished STAR stories (all under 3 min)
- [ ] 2-min self-intro rehearsed
- [ ] "Why Google?" answer authentic and specific
- [ ] Can answer any common behavioral question by mapping to a story
- [ ] 5 questions to ask interviewers prepared
- [ ] Practiced follow-up answers for each story
- [ ] Know what Googleyness means and can demonstrate it

**Final exercise**: Record yourself answering 3 random behavioral questions. Watch the recording. Critique.
